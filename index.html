Our Mission

YSJOI - by the youth, for the youth of Iraq!

In the great land that was once Mesopotamia, and the home of the hanging gardens of Babylon, progress in science has slowed.

Why?

Because the youth of Iraq have no platform to build upon. Opportunities to learn about science, technology, engineering, and mathematics are rare, and students trying to learn about them are even actively discouraged.

But instead of surrendering to that silence, we build our own base.

And that first step is YSJOI — a place to teach, learn, and explore.

— From the Founding Speech of Satvik M. Bhure

About YSJOI

The Youth Science Journal of Iraq (YSJOI) is a student-led scientific publication created to give young minds in Iraq a platform to explore, question, and contribute to the world of STEM.

Each month, we publish selected questions, researched answers, and student contributions across physics, mathematics, biology, engineering, and beyond.

Whether you are here to read, ask, or contribute — you are part of the next generation shaping Iraq’s scientific future.
